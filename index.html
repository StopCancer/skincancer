Skin Cancer

Skin cancer is the most common of all cancers. About 3.5 million people are diagnosed in the United States anually. Melanoma is a more dangerous skin cancer, which causes 73,000 deaths anually. There are many different types of cancer that all have a big impact on people's lives. To reduce your risk at this cancer, follow these steps.




Don’t Become Overweight: Studies have proven that obesity can increase your risk of cancer. Try your best to avoid gaining weight. It is safest to keep your B.M.I. (body mass index) under 25. Use this calculator to see your B.M.I Find Your B.M.I.



Eat Healthy:Have a good balanced diet not only decreases your risk of cancer,but helps you succeed in staying at a reasonable weight. Your balanced diet should include vegetables, fruits, and whole grains. Try to stay away from sugary drinks, fatty foods, and red meat.



Keep Active: Actively exercising can decrease your cancer risk from ten to thirty percent! You can still moderately exercise and still get the same results. It is recommended to exercise either 150 minutes moderately, or 75 minutes vigorously per week.



Limit Alcohol Intake: Drinking alcohol can increase your breast can sufficiently increase your risk of breast cancer. Even small amounts can increase your breast cancer risk, and it is better to limit 1 alcoholic beverage per day.



Don’t Smoke: Studies have shown that long-term smoking increases your risk of breast cancer. Not smoking also helps decrease a variety of other cancers.



Remove Your Breasts: This can be a big sacrifice for some people, but it is the most sufficient. This will decrease your risk by a whole 90%!
